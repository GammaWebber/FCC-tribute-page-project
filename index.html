Sanusi Lamido Sanusi

Emir of Kano State

Emir of Kano Sanusi Lamido Sanusi II

Here's a summarized timeline of Sanusi Lamido Sanusi with accurate dates:

Early Life and Education

  • July 31, 1961: Born in Kano, Nigeria
  • 1981: Graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Economics from Ahmadu Bello University in Zaria, Nigeria.
  • 1983: Obtained a Master's degree in Economics from the same institution.

Banking Career

  • 1985: Joined Icon Limited (Merchant Bankers), a subsidiary of Barings Bank.
  • 1997: Joined the United Bank for Africa (UBA) as a Principal Manager in the Credit and Risk Management Division.
  • 2005: Appointed Executive Director of Risk and Management Control at First Bank of Nigeria.
  • 2009: Became Group Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer of First Bank of Nigeria.

Governor of the Central Bank of Nigeria

  • June 3, 2009: Appointed Governor of the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) by President Umaru Musa Yar'Adua.
  • 2010: Named Central Bank Governor of the Year by The Banker magazine.
  • 2011: Awarded the title of Commander of the Order of the Niger (CON).

Controversies and Suspension

  • 2013: Alleged that the Nigerian National Petroleum Corporation (NNPC) failed to remit $20 billion to the government.
  • February 20, 2014: Suspended as CBN Governor by President Goodluck Jonathan.

Emir of Kano

  • June 8, 2014: Appointed as the Emir of Kano, becoming the 14th Fulani Emir of Kano.
  • 2015: Officially crowned as the Emir of Kano.

Deposition and Later Life

  • March 9, 2020: Deposed as Emir of Kano by Governor Abdullahi Umar Ganduje of Kano State, citing insubordination.
  • March 2020: Relocated to Nasarawa State following his deposition.
  • May 2024: reinstated as the Emir of Kano State by Governor Abba Kabir Yusuf.

Sanusi Lamido Sanusi's career has been marked by significant achievements in banking and finance, as well as notable controversies and political challenges.
